# Week 2 Analysis

## Learning Points

### User Stories and Prioritization

The team learned that not all requested features belong in the first release. MoSCoW prioritization helped identify the core functionality required for MVP v1 and postpone lower-priority features.

Writing user stories also revealed dependencies between features, particularly around vehicle registration, timeline management, and AI assistance.

### Prototyping and Interface Design

Creating prototypes helped transform customer requirements into concrete user workflows. The team identified the vehicle timeline as the central element of the application and refined navigation based on customer feedback.

Developing multiple interface concepts before selecting a final design improved the overall solution quality.

### MVP v0 Deployment

The MVP v0 work confirmed that the selected technology stack and repository structure provide a suitable foundation for future development.
    
The team also gained experience organizing development through Git branches and collaborative workflows.

---

## Validated Assumptions

### Confirmed

* Users need a centralized vehicle history timeline.
* A mobile application is the appropriate primary interface.
* AI assistance can simplify vehicle management workflows.
* Timeline records should be presented chronologically.
* The proposed technical architecture is feasible.

### Modified

* AI should support the timeline workflow rather than replace it.
* Several advanced features were moved outside the MVP v1 scope after prioritization.

---

## Needs Clarification

The following topics require further discussion with the customer:

* Vehicle ownership transfer process.
* Support for transportation types other than cars.
* Level of AI automation for creating records.
* Data validation requirements.
* Future external integrations.

Technical risks include AI service costs, response quality, and long-term scalability.

---

## Planned Response

For MVP v1, the team will focus on Must Have user stories, maintain the timeline as the primary application workflow, and continue validating requirements with the customer before expanding functionality.
